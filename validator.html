frequently asked questions

Who is a Validator?

Proof-Of-Stake blockchains rely on validators to secure the network. Validators run nodes and participate in the consensus protocol by committing new blocks in the blockchain and receive commission in return. Validators also participate in governance of decentralized networks by voting on proposals for the betterment of the network.

What is staking?

Staking is the act of delegating your crypto assets to a validator. When you stake your tokens with a validator, they are “bonded”, meaning locked, for a set amount of time that differs from blockchain to blockchain. While staked, you earn blockchain rewards and are eligible to airdrops. Your tokens are still yours; they remain in your wallet and in your custody, only the right to participate in the consensus of the blockchain with your staked tokens is delegated to the validator.

How can I stake with Scrib Code?

You can stake by going to any network we support, select Scrib Code validator and stake your tokens. Remember when you delegate, you start earning rewards! Thanks for your support.

Can I lose my crypto asset by staking?

You will hardly lose your asset by staking. You can only lose if the node/validator is not working properly and gets jailed. Crypto asset you stake remains yours and we at Scrib Code don't have access to it.

Can I withdraw my crypto asset any time i want?

You can always withdraw your delegated crypto asset anytime, but it will take some days to process depending on the network. Usually is around 14 days but can take more.

Can I re-delegate my crypto asset to another validator whenever i want?

Yes, most of the networks allows you to re-delegate your tokens without having to wait. This is perfect to keep delegation power split between all validators. No validator should have the majority of power as that can compromise governance/voting system and the project!

How do I earn rewards when i delegate my crypto asset?

Proof-of-stake, aims to promote staking to validators so you keep the network alive while still holding to your tokens. This mechanism allows you to earn a % of rewards for the tokens you delegate and gives you access to most airdrops.

Why do validators take "commission"?

Validators take commission to help them cover costs of running the nodes/network. We will always charge you the average commission. Some nodes have 0% commission, but there's a possibility you can run into problems with that, they also make it unfair for others as running validators do have a cost. They are also excluded from airdrops most of the times.
